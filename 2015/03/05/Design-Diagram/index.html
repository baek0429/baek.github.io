Rethinking the curb-road system.

Why the Curb?

Planners and designers have been using the curb-road system for too long without thinking about its contemporary meaning in the street. However, the recent diversification of tranportation modees calls for the new definition about the road-curb system in the modern street.
To redefine the meaning of the curb, it is important to ask the right question.

The Rise of the Curb

I would begin with the origin of the modern curb in 1860s, London. It was the imporatn plan of the city beautification movement led by the local government. The curb was considered as a symbol of modern street due to the clear distinction between dirty public street and organized pedestrian path. A decade later, the design principle was imported to other cities in Europe. American cities also started to adopt the curb-road system in the popular street.

Adding Layers on top of the Curb

It was the eary 20th century that the first local ordinance was set upon the curb to refrain the movement of the pedestrian in the street. As automobile became the main transportation over the horse, the perspective that the public street belongs to automobile became persuasive.

Argument

My Argument regarding the meaning of the curb in the street is that the curb is the boundary between the options that pedestrian can choose, not the only option.
